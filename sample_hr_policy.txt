EMPLOYEE HR POLICY HANDBOOK

1. LEAVE POLICY
Employees are entitled to 18 days of paid annual leave per calendar year, accrued monthly
at 1.5 days per month. Sick leave is provided separately at 12 days per year and does not
require advance notice, though a medical certificate is required for sick leave exceeding
3 consecutive days. Casual leave of 6 days per year may be used for personal emergencies
with at least 1 day of prior notice where possible. Unused annual leave can be carried
forward up to a maximum of 15 days into the next calendar year; beyond that, it lapses.
Maternity leave is provided for 26 weeks as per company policy, and paternity leave is
provided for 2 weeks. All leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal and
approved by the reporting manager at least 2 working days in advance, except for sick leave.

2. WORK HOURS AND ATTENDANCE
Standard work hours are 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, Monday through Friday, with a 1-hour lunch
break. Employees are expected to log a minimum of 8 working hours per day. The company
follows a hybrid work model, requiring employees to be present in the office at least
3 days per week, with the remaining days eligible for remote work subject to manager
approval. Flexible start times between 8:30 AM and 10:30 AM are permitted as long as
total daily hours are met. Repeated unexcused absences may result in disciplinary action
as outlined in the Code of Conduct section.

3. EMPLOYEE BENEFITS
All full-time employees are eligible for group health insurance covering the employee,
spouse, and up to 2 dependent children, with coverage up to 5 lakh rupees per year.
Dental and vision coverage are included as add-ons at no additional cost to the employee.
The company also provides a wellness allowance of 5000 rupees per quarter, which can be
used for gym memberships, mental health counseling, or fitness programs. Employees
become eligible for benefits from day one of employment, with no waiting period.
Life insurance coverage equal to 3 times the annual salary is provided at no cost.

4. REMOTE WORK POLICY
Employees working remotely must ensure a stable internet connection and be available
during core business hours from 11 AM to 4 PM for meetings and collaboration. Remote
work equipment, including a laptop and one-time setup allowance of 10000 rupees, is
provided to employees who are approved for extended remote work arrangements. Remote
employees are expected to attend all-hands meetings virtually and are eligible for the
same benefits as in-office employees. Requests for full-time remote work beyond the
standard hybrid model must be approved by both the reporting manager and HR.

5. CODE OF CONDUCT AND DISCIPLINARY POLICY
Employees are expected to maintain professionalism, respect diversity, and avoid conflicts
of interest. Harassment of any kind is strictly prohibited and can be reported confidentially
through the HR ethics hotline. Violations of company policy may result in a verbal warning,
written warning, or termination depending on severity, following a structured escalation
process. All employees must complete mandatory compliance and ethics training annually.

6. RESIGNATION AND NOTICE PERIOD
Employees who wish to resign must provide a minimum of 30 days written notice to their
reporting manager and HR. During the notice period, employees are required to complete
a knowledge transfer with their team. Full and final settlement, including any pending
reimbursements and leave encashment, is processed within 45 days of the last working day.
Leave encashment is calculated based on unused annual leave balance at the basic salary rate.
